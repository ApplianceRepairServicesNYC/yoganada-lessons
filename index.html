The Teacher

Paramahansa Yogananda

Paramahansa Yogananda (1893‚Äì1952) transformed Western understanding of Eastern wisdom. His teachings on self-mastery, discipline, and awareness have guided millions toward personal transformation and peak performance.

His book "Autobiography of a Yogi" has sold millions of copies worldwide and was the only book Steve Jobs had on his iPad‚Äîread once every year.

Born Mukunda Lal Ghosh in Gorakhpur, India, Yogananda showed spiritual inclinations from childhood. He met his guru Sri Yukteswar in 1910 and spent a decade mastering ancient techniques. In 1920, he traveled to America, becoming one of the first Indian teachers to settle in the West, where he founded Self-Realization Fellowship.
Yogananda lectured to capacity crowds across America for over 30 years. He met with presidents, scientists, and cultural leaders. His teachings influenced figures from George Harrison to Steve Jobs. Today, Self-Realization Fellowship has centers worldwide and his book remains a perennial bestseller.
Yogananda taught that lasting happiness comes from within through scientific techniques of concentration and meditation. His approach emphasizes direct experience over blind belief, practical application over theory, and balanced living that honors both material and spiritual needs.
In an age of distraction and anxiety, Yogananda's practical methods for developing focus, discipline, and inner peace are more relevant than ever. His techniques require no religious belief‚Äîthey work through understanding the mechanics of mind and applying systematic practices.
